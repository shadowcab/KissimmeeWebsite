Main Street and Downtown Kissimmee

Downtown Kissimmee Murals

Osceola Arts sponsors a program called ARTisNOW, a public mural project. Their mission is to create a collaboration between local mural artists and community leaders to transform the downtown Kissimmee area with colorful, vibrant murals. You can view these murals all along Broadway and its side streets. In 2021, they are expanding the program to include the area from downtown to the Vine Street Corridor.

    • This mural can be found on the side of the private building at 407 Broadway. The building was once owned or leased by Art in Motion, LLC, but they no longer occupy the building. I have no other information. This mural stood out to me by the way the artist incorporated the actual door of the building into the mural.

    • Downtown Kissimmee also offers a large collection of outdoor public sculptures. This one is called Angel of the Caribbean by Edmundo Lopez. You can find it in front of City Hall at 101 Church Street, Kissimmee, FL 34741.

    • This is a permanent sculpture dedicated to Bette Sprinkle, the "First Lady of Osceola County". The artist is Sandra Storm.

    • This is Shuggah Momma with Shuggah Baby. Artist is unknown. It's located on the corner of Monument Ave and Broadway Ave.
