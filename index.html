Introduction

About...

Kissimmee, Florida is a city with a rich history and culture. But outside of Central Florida, it is probably best known for its “off-Disney” resort rates, cheap souvenirs, and Tourist District (aka Old Town). But if you look closer, you’ll find a relatively small city with its own identity beyond the “mouse ears”. This website is about the city of Kissimmee, Florida, and a few of its offerings for history buffs, street art enthusiasts, and small festival event seekers.

As a first stop, I highly recommend the Osceola County Welcome Center and History Museum (4155 W. Vine St. Kissimmee FL 34741). The admission is free. See slideshow below. There are several realistic dioramas that present the history of Kissimmee and its timeline from the early citrus and cattle industries to a thriving transportation hub and later as a tourist destination.

Fun Facts...

Kissimmee falls within Osceola County, south of Orlando. It has a population of 71,185 (2019) with a 56.3% Democrat public. It operates under a Council-Manager form of government where its elected officials work alongside a business-oriented City Manager who oversees the city’s various departments. (City Government | City of Kissimmee, FL, n.d.)

The city name itself is believed to be a direct translation from the Jororo tribal word “Cacema” which means “long water”. This reference is to Lake Tohopekaliga (aka Lake Toho) which is the largest lake in Osceola County. Lake Toho covers 22,700 acres and has a circumference of 42 miles. The lake is popular for its tournament bass fishing. Other fresh water fish found in Lake Toho include Black crappie, Largemouth bass, Spotted bass, Bluegill, and Walleye. (Granicus, n.d.)
